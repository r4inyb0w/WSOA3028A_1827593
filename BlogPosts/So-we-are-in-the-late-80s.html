So we are in the late 80s?

Friday, 24 April

This website is how I imagine a “modern web” would be used in the late 80s if they had access to the same technologies we do. I believe information that was kept from South Africans would be easier to access with this web. Additionally, they would be able to access information from other countries that would otherwise be prohibited. For example, anti-apartheid rallies and movements in Britain, like the AAM or Boycott Movement [1] would not have been shown to South Africans in that time, however with this technology I believe this information could have been accessed, even if South Africa had limited their citizens’ access. We know that China has heavy internet censorship, reportedly 18 000 websites are blocked [2], however it is suggested that citizens still find ways to access this blocked information. This is apparently done through the use of VPNs or changing IP addresses [2]. I believe if this was the case during the Apartheid era, activists would find a way to access this information.

Why did I choose this timeframe?

Since my parents were in their 20s in the 80s, I felt like I could attain information from a primary source, which would make my site more believable. The late 80s also includes the Purple March. Since my character is a florist and a creative person, I felt linking her activism to events characterized by colours opened the doors to display her creativity.

Why did I choose this character?

I am a girl.

I am in university.

I am in my 20s.

Macey Daniels is a reflection of myself. It’s easier to develop a character that I can relate to. She is also someone I aspire to be like. She runs a business, she is family oriented and she sticks to her beliefs.

I also thought if I wanted to write about an activist, I would want to write about her in a significant context. Not to say that activism isn’t relevant all the time, but I thought using one of the biggest historical tragedies of South Africa would be more understandable.

I could have set this character in modern times and have her discuss the issues we as South Africans are facing now, however it was more of challenge for me to write about an earlier time since I needed to do more research and determine what information was around in that time and how internet access and these modern technologies would have influenced her and her way of thinking.
